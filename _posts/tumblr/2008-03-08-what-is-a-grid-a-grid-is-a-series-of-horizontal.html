What is a Grid?

A grid is a series of horizontal and vertical lines that sub-divide a page into smaller areas. (A ‘page’ does not have to be a printed page. Grids are used in a website design and computer-based applications.) Grids can be derived solely by mathematics, allowing equations and ratios to determine proportion, or a combination of simple division of the overall page dimensions customized for solving the problem at hand. Investigate: The Golden Ratio a.k.a. The Golden Mean Regardless of how it is devised, a typographic grid organizes text and images across the pages of a document. A grid can consist of a single column framed by margins, or it may have multiple columns and rows. (via Learn: GRPH 210 + Typography)

Cartier-Bresson visited Russia in 1954 and his report on its people is arguably his greatest essay in this genre. (via The People of Russia, Henri Cartier-Bresson | openDemocracy)

This image is a spread from Henri Cartier-Bresson’s “The People of Russia”, originally published in Paris Match (France)in 1955. The image is taken from “Things As They Are : Photojournalism in Context since 1955”, which tells the story of reportage photography from the heyday of Life magazine to the era of the camera-phone in the present day.

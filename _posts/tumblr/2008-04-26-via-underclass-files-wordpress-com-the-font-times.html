The font Times New Roman was created in 1931 by Stanley Morison together with Starling Burgess and Victor Lardent and has been an influential font during and before the digital typeface era. It has also inspired others including Georgia which has a wider serif.

Times New Roman has been re-designed from old-style characteristics and adapted to give better legibility for its main use in books and magazines, for reports, office documents and also for display and advertising.

Takako Saito, Spice Chess (1965)Â 

"Spice Chess" (Fig. 5), a special subset of "Smell Chess," featured different spices in test tubes as the various pieces; the players would have to learn to identify the pieces by not only vision (color and texture) but also by smell. While Saito did not dismantle the playing of the game of chess, she reminded the viewer that other senses can also be drawn upon or made to figure in the most analytical and competitive tasks or games. (via Reconstruction 6.1 (Winter 2006))

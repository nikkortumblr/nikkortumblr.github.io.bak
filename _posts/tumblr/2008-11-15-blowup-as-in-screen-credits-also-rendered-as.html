Blowup (as in screen credits, also rendered as Blow-Up) is a 1966 British-Italian art film directed by Michelangelo Antonioni and was that director’s first English language film. It tells the story of a photographer’s involvement with a murder case. The film was inspired by the short story “Las Babas del Diablo” (“The Droolings of the Devil”) by Argentinian writer Julio Cortázar, and by the work, habits, and mannerisms of Swinging London photographer David Bailey. The film was scored by jazz pianist Herbie Hancock, although the music is diegetic as it is played on a record by the main character. Though nominated for several awards, it failed to win anything but the Grand Prix at the Cannes Film Festival. (via Blowup - Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia)

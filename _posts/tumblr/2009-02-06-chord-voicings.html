- “So What” voicing: major third on top, quartal stack below

- “Kenny Barron” m11 voicing: two perfect 5ths in each hand, separated by minor second (1-5-9-b3-7-11)

- “Killer Joe” dom7 voicing: 7-3-13-9-5-1

- “Bud Powell” voicings: 1-7, 1-3 

- “Bill Evans” voicings: seems like this is sometimes loosely used to refer to the typical LH voicings with 3 or 7 on the bottom (aka the “A position” and “B position” voicings)

- “Frank Mantooth” voicings (there are several in his Voicings book; mostly pure quartal stacks, “So What” voicings, or upper-structure RH triads over a dom7 LH shell; I’m not sure which of these the phrase “Mantooth voicing” really is intended to refer to)

- McCoy Tyner’s name is often associated with quartal stacks.

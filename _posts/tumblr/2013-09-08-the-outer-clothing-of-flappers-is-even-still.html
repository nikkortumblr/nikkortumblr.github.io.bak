The outer clothing of flappers is even still extremely identifiable. This look, called “garconne” (“little boy”), was instigated by Coco Chanel.8 To look more like a boy, women tightly wound their chest with strips of cloth in order to flatten it.9 The waists of flapper clothes were dropped to the hipline. She wore stockings - made of rayon (“artificial silk”) starting in 1923 - which the flapper often wore rolled over a garter belt.
—Flappers in the Roaring Twenties

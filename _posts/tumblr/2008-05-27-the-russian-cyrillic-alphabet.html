  • А а - a as in father
  • Р р - r as in error
  • Б б - b as in but
  • С с - s as in smile
  • В в - v as in van
  • Т т - t as in ten
  • Г г - g as in get
  • У у - u as in cool
  • Д д - d as in dress
  • Ф ф - f as in farm
  • Е е - ye as in yesterday
  • Х х - h as in loch
  • Ё ё - yo as in yonder
  • Ц ц - ts as in its
  • Ж ж - zh as in measure
  • Ч ч - ch as in chess
  • З з - z as in zoo
  • Ш ш - sh as in fish
  • И и - ee as in meet
  • Щ щ - shsh as in fresh chicken
  • Й й - y as in toy
  • Ъ ъ - the “hard sign”, has no sound of its own, used to separate a consonant from a following sound, effect is just a brief pause
  • К к - k as in class
  • Л л - l as in love
  • Ы ы - i as in bill (with the tongue tip a little bit further back)
  • М м - m as mother
  • Ь ь - the “soft sign”, makes the preceding consonant soft like the first n in “onion”
  • Н н - n as in name
  • Э э - e as in bet
  • О о - o as in bottle
  • Ю ю - yu as in Yugoslavia
  • П п - p as in paper
  • Я я - ya as in yard
